<p><ul><li>Cellulose Base Membrane</li><li>Synthetic Polymer Membrane</li></ul></p>
<p><p>The hemodialysis membrane market is primarily divided into two types: cellulose-based membranes and synthetic polymer membranes. Cellulose-based membranes, derived from natural cellulose, are known for their biocompatibility and affordability but may have limitations in performance. In contrast, synthetic polymer membranes are engineered from various polymers, offering enhanced flexibility, resistance to fouling, and improved solute removal efficiency. The choice between these membrane types influences treatment efficacy, patient outcomes, and costs in hemodialysis procedures, catering to diverse medical needs and preferences.</p></p>
