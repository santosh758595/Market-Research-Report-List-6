<p><p>An interposer is a semiconductor component that serves to connect integrated circuits (ICs) in a multi-chip module. <p><p>The interposer market comprises three main types: 2D, 2.5D, and 3D interposers. </p><p>- **2D interposers** serve as a flat substrate connecting multiple chips, enhancing signal integrity and reducing packaging complexity. </p><p>- **2.5D interposers** introduce a vertical layer, facilitating higher-density chip integration while maintaining lower power consumption. </p><p>- **3D interposers** stack chips to significantly optimize space and performance, allowing for advanced functionalities and improved thermal management. Each type addresses specific application needs in the semiconductor landscape.</p></p>
