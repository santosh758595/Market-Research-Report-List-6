<p><p>Real-Time Clock (RTC) ICs are integrated circuits that keep track of the current time and date, even when the main power is turned off. They maintain time accuracy using a quartz crystal oscillator and are often equipped with additional features such as alarm functions and battery backup. RTC ICs are utilized in various applications including consumer electronics, automotive systems, industrial equipment, and smart grid technologies.</p><p>The RTC ICs market is experiencing significant growth, driven by the increasing demand for precise timekeeping in a plethora of devices. The rise of the Internet of Things (IoT) and smart devices is also bolstering the market, as these applications often require reliable time management for synchronization and coordination. Furthermore, the expansion of automotive electronics with advanced driver-assistance systems (ADAS) and infotainment solutions is contributing to the evolving landscape of the RTC market.</p><p>As technology advances, RTC ICs are becoming more sophisticated, integrating functionalities that cater to power efficiency and miniaturization needs.
