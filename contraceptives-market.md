<p><p>The contraceptives market is categorized into three main types: prolonged contraception, short-term contraception, and emergency contraception. Prolonged contraception includes methods like implants and intrauterine devices (IUDs) that provide extended protection, usually lasting for several years. Short-term contraception encompasses options such as pills, patches, and condoms, effective for a shorter duration, typically within a month or per sexual encounter. Emergency contraception refers to methods used after unprotected intercourse, providing a last-minute option to prevent pregnancy, typically effective within 72 to 120 hours.</p></p>
