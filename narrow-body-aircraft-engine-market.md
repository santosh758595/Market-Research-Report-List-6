Turbofan engines, characterized by high bypass ratios, are favored for their fuel efficiency and reduced noise, making them ideal for commercial jetliners. Conversely, turboprop engines use a propeller and are generally more efficient at lower speeds and altitudes, often serving regional and short-haul flights. Each engine type caters to different operational needs, driving their respective market dynamics and preferences among airlines.</p></p>
