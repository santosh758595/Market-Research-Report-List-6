<p><p>Wrist-worn pulse oximeters measure blood oxygen levels and heart rate, with two main market types: Separate Sensor and Integrated Sensor. Separate Sensor devices use a detachable sensor, often clipped to a finger, connected to the wrist unit, offering flexibility and higher accuracy. Integrated Sensor models have the sensor built into the wristband, providing convenience and portability but may sacrifice some precision. Both types cater to health monitoring needs, with trade-offs between accuracy and ease of use.</p></p>
