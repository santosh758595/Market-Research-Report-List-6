<p><p>Prenatal testing equipment is categorized based on the trimester in which the testing is conducted. First trimester testing involves screening for chromosomal abnormalities, such as Down syndrome, between 10-13 weeks of pregnancy. Second trimester testing includes more in-depth screenings for genetic disorders and birth defects, usually done between 15-20 weeks. Third trimester testing focuses on assessing the health and development of the baby in preparation for birth. Post-term testing is done after the due date to monitor the baby's well-being and decide if induction is necessary.</p></p>
