<p><p>The PEM fuel cell catalysts market is divided into two primary types: platinum-based and non-platinum catalysts. Platinum-based catalysts are the most efficient and widely used, offering high performance and stability in electrochemical reactions. However, their high cost and limited availability pose challenges. Non-platinum catalysts, such as transition metal alloys and carbon-based materials, provide a more economical alternative, albeit with generally lower efficiency. The market is shifting towards non-platinum options to reduce costs and improve sustainability in fuel cell technologies.</p></p>
