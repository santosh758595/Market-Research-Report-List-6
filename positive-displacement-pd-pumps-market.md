Reciprocating pumps use a back-and-forth motion to move fluids, making them suitable for high-pressure applications. Rotary pumps, on the other hand, employ rotating mechanisms to move liquids, ideal for continuous flow and lower viscosity fluids. Other types may include diaphragm and gear pumps, each with specific applications across industries such as oil and gas, water treatment, and chemical processing.</p></p>
