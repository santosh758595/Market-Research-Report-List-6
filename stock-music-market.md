<p><p>The stock music market is primarily divided into two types: royalty-free (RF) and rights-managed (RM). In the RF market, users pay a one-time fee for unlimited usage without ongoing royalties, making it a convenient option for creators. Conversely, the RM market offers music based on specific licensing terms, where fees vary depending on usage, duration, and distribution scope. RM tends to provide exclusivity and higher quality, appealing to projects with unique or high-profile needs.</p></p>
