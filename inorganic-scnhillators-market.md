<p><p>Inorganic scintillators are materials that efficiently convert ionizing radiation into visible light, used in radiation detection and imaging. Sodium Iodide (NaI) is known for its high light output and sensitivity, making it ideal for gamma spectroscopy. Cesium Iodide (CsI) offers superior density and moisture resistance, enhancing performance in medical imaging. Gadolinium Oxysulfide (GOS) is utilized for its excellent energy resolution and is commonly used in X-ray imaging systems. Other scintillators may include materials like Barium Fluoride and Lead Tungstate, each serving specialized applications.</p></p>
